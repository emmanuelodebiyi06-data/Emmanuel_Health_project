# Global Health & Mortality Analytics Dashboard (1900–2020)

## Project Overview
This project is an end-to-end **Power BI** solution developed to analyze a massive global health dataset spanning over a century. The dashboard provides a "gangan" (major) deep-dive into how population shifts, medical doctor availability, and healthcare expenditure correlate with mortality rates across 7 continents.

## Key Insights & Global Trends
* **Mortality Magnitude**: Captured and visualized a total of **2.85 Billion deaths** recorded between 1990 and 2019, with India ($0.24bn$) and China ($0.18bn$) representing the highest volumes.
* **Demographic Vulnerability**: Identified that the **70+ age group** accounts for the largest share of global deaths (**42.99%**), highlighting the impact of long-term illnesses.
* **Healthcare Disparities**: Analyzed the "Doctor Density" metric, revealing a global average of only **1.86 doctors per 10,000 people**, with significant gaps between European and African regions.
* **Population Surge**: Tracked the historical growth of the global population, peaking at **2.41 Trillion** in the early 1900s baseline.

## Technical Implementation (Power BI)
* **Data Modeling**: Built from scratch using complex datasets covering 266 entities and 7 continents.
* **DAX Measures**: Created custom calculations for Average Expenditure ($0.01\%$ of GDP) and Mortality Ratios.
* **Advanced Visuals**:
    * **Donut Charts**: For age-group mortality distribution.
    * **Time-Series Line Graphs**: Tracking 5-year trends in medical professional availability.
    * **KPI Cards**: For instant visibility into high-level global metrics.
